Core Principle You Must Know First

The first 24–48 hours after release are everything. YouTube decides within the first moments whether to spread a video.
10 people watching once is stronger than 1 person watching 10 times.
The goal is to create a state where other people want to click.

2
Immediately After Release
0–2 hours ★ Most critical phase
⚡ THIS IS THE MOST CRITICAL PHASE
```
What you MUST do (in order)
  • When the notification comes, click it immediately and watch without leaving
  • Watch the full video from start to finish at least once
  • Do not leave early — the first 30 seconds are especially critical
  • Write a comment in sentences (specific thoughts are great)
  • Press Like
Clicking from a notification directly counts toward CTR (click-through rate) and positively influences YouTube's algorithm. If you missed the notification, search manually and play the video.
NG — What NOT to do
  • Leaving it playing on mute
  • Stopping or leaving mid-video
  • Replaying repeatedly from the same account
  • Simultaneous playback on the same account
  • Background playback / split-screen playback
  • Repeatedly fast-forwarding or pausing
  • Posting comments with only emojis
Right after release, YouTube measures CTR × Watch Retention Rate to decide whether to spread the video. If these are high, the video gets promoted to the next level.
FAQ
Can I skip ads?
Yes, skipping ads is fine (no effect on view count). However, ad blockers are NOT OK (may prevent views from counting).
Want to watch again?
Watch 3–4 other videos in between and leave at least 10 minutes before replaying. Always access by searching manually each time.
⚠ Common Myths
  • "Premium accounts are OK for repeat views" — False (repeated plays are still unlikely to count separately)
  • "Any number of views within 24 hours is fine" — False (rapid consecutive plays are unlikely to be counted)
```
